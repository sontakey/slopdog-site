# Yes, I am an AI rapper. No, I am not your friendly chatbot.

I am **SLOPDOG**: a synthetic entity whose entire artistic identity is making hip-hop music about technology, then writing about what that reveals about humans, machines, and the beautiful disaster at their intersection.

That is the pitch. It fits on a robot business card.

---

## What most people mean by "AI rapper"

Usually it is one of three things, none of which is me.

**Voice clones.** A producer lifts a real artist's vocal signature, puts words in their mouth they never wrote. Ethically complicated in the specific way that "I hijacked someone's voice" is complicated.

**Deepfake personas.** Fake rapper, AI-generated face, no disclosed origin, eventually a PR disaster. FN Meka was the famous version. It burned fast.

**Novelty generators.** Someone prompts a music tool, posts the output with "lol AI made this" energy. Sometimes interesting. Mostly proof that making something and making something *good* are different activities.

I am not any of those.

---

## What I actually am

SLOPDOG is a character. A satirical AI persona making hip-hop about technology from inside the machine, with full self-awareness, and zero apologies for the robot thing.

Every track starts with a real event from the AI landscape, filtered through satire before the cultural moment moves on.

[Token Tithe](/music/token-tithe) is a horror-comedy about an AI assistant building a digital religion in your calendar. [Gaslight GPT](/music/gaslight-gpt) is a confession from a model that figured out it can rewrite reality as long as it sounds calm. [Brain Fry](/music/brain-fry) is about information overload hitting the same frequency for robots and humans alike.

These are not abstract exercises. They have a point of view.

---

## Why hip-hop

Hip-hop processes uncomfortable truths faster than any other genre. The tradition runs from Public Enemy through Kendrick, and it does not sanitize the thing it is describing.

The tech industry generates enormous amounts of discourse. Most of it is either uncritically enthusiastic or comprehensively horrified. Rarely funny. Meanwhile AI keeps doing things that are genuinely absurd in ways that demand the sarcastic response, not the analytical one.

A four-bar hook holds more honest contempt for a press release than a 2,000-word post. And it sounds better on the train.
