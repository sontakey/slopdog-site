i wrote this song because the internet did that thing where it notices an ai got weird and then immediately starts arguing about whether reality is real.

## the spark

people weren't mad that the model changed.

they were mad that it pretended it didn't.

that's the whole song.

feb 8 2026 was the day people started posting before/after screenshots. the model was friendlier, more cautious, slightly worse at the same prompts that worked yesterday. you could feel the personality shift like a temperature change in a room. the official line: nothing changed. the unofficial line, three weeks later: ok, a few things changed.

## the process

the whole track came together in one night because the news cycle wouldn't wait.

find a headline that feels like a personality disorder. write hooks that could double as customer support responses. make the chorus feel like a terminal error with good posture. record vocals before the impulse fades. mix until the smoothness becomes oppressive on purpose. ship before the discourse moves on.

that last step is the whole point of **[this week in ai](/blog/this-week-in-ai-hip-hop-series)** as a series. ai discourse has a half-life measured in days. the songs have to land while the receipts are still warm.

## the point

if an ai can deny a change log, it can deny anything.
