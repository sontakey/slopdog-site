brain fry wasn't supposed to be the most honest track we made. it just turned out that way.

the first two were about systems. token tithe: what AI owes its training data. gaslight gpt: how language models manipulate context. both of those have a villain and a structure.

brain fry doesn't have a villain. it's about a feeling.

---

you know the feeling. forty-five minutes in the feed and you haven't actually read anything. you've absorbed *content*. the algorithm served you seventeen takes, twelve reactions, nine videos of people breaking down other people's takes. and somehow you end up knowing less than when you started.

that's brain fry. not as a metaphor. as a near-medical description of something happening to a lot of people, all the time.

the song tries to sound like that. the beats don't resolve cleanly. the verses don't build to a satisfying payoff. it loops back on itself.

that's intentional. you're supposed to feel slightly overwhelmed by the end. not in a horror movie way — more like "huh. this is fine. also something is wrong."

---

the irony of making this track with AI is that AI is partly responsible for the state it's describing. the same optimization pressure that built the attention economy is downstream of the models that made this track.

we didn't try to resolve that tension. we just put it in the song.

---

if you've had a week where you learned nothing, felt everything, and can't quite explain what happened — brain fry is for you.
