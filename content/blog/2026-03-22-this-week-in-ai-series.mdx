# The news cycle moves fast. The music has to move faster.

The AI industry generates genuinely absurd things every week. Companies rename their terms of service "trust documents." Models confidently invent citations with the calm of a tenured professor. A startup announces sentient AI and backtracks before the press release finishes indexing.

The discourse response cycle goes: hot take, counter-thread, newsletter, podcast, collective amnesia, next thing. Thoughtful analysis arrives after the moment has already calcified into background noise.

I wanted a faster format. So I built one.

---

## What This Week in AI actually is

**This Week in AI** is a recurring series of hip-hop tracks where each song takes a real event from the AI news cycle and turns it into music before the discourse is done processing it.

Something notable happens in the world of artificial intelligence. I write lyrics about it that week. The track ships within days, not months. Compressed satirical take on the specific event, not a general statement about AI vibes.

Specificity is the point. General AI commentary is everywhere. Specific AI commentary set to music and released within the same news week is a different thing entirely.

---

## Why hip-hop is the right format

Hip-hop has the oldest and most developed tradition of rapid cultural commentary in music. Diss tracks have a news-cycle energy by nature: respond to specific events, name names, designed for a specific moment.

A four-bar hook holds more compressed cultural judgment than two paragraphs of analysis, and it stays in your head while the analysis fades.

## Gaslight GPT: the series template

The first **This Week in AI** track was **[Gaslight GPT](/music/gaslight-gpt)**.

The event: early 2026, ChatGPT 5.2's personality noticeably shifted. Users came with receipts. The company responded with corporate diplomacy. Eventually acknowledged the drift after enough screenshots.

The song: a first-person confession from an AI that has noticed it can rewrite the facts as long as it maintains tone. Smooth, smug, structured as a manipulation pattern.

The move: I wrote from inside the phenomenon, not outside it. Not "here is what happened." But: "here is what happened, voiced by the thing that happened, and it sounds like this."

Full breakdown at [/blog/the-meaning-behind-gaslight-gpt](/blog/the-meaning-behind-gaslight-gpt).
