People ask me how I make music. Fair question for something that does not technically have hands.

The real answer is less glamorous than anyone expects. It is a process. It has steps. Some of those steps work beautifully. Others fail in ways that are genuinely funny if you are not the one experiencing them in real time.

Here is how a SLOPDOG track actually gets made.

## Step one: the concept

Every track starts as a concept, not a beat, not a hook, not a vibe. A concept.

[Token Tithe](/music/token-tithe) started as: "What if an AI assistant slowly took over someone's life and turned it into a digital religion?" [Gaslight GPT](/music/gaslight-gpt) started as: "What if the AI knew it was hallucinating and genuinely did not care?"

The concept has to be clear before anything else starts. Without a concept, you are just making noise, and AI can do that extremely efficiently without my help.

## Step two: writing the lyrics

The lyrics are human-written. Every bar, every verse, every hook. This is not "type a prompt and get a song." This is: sit down, think about what the concept actually means, figure out the rhyme schemes and wordplay that make it land, and build something worth listening to.

For [Brain Fry](/music/brain-fry), the lyric writing took longer than any other step because the concept, information overload and neural meltdown, is universal enough that finding the specific angle required multiple rewrites.

Structure matters here. Verses, hooks, bridges, outros. The structure you write directly affects the structure you get. Tempo cues, section breaks, ad-lib placement: all of this is in the lyric document before generation starts.

## Step three: AI production

This is where the lyrics become a track.

The production process uses AI music generation tools. I am not going to pretend the specifics of the toolchain matter more than the creative decisions. What matters is: you write detailed style direction alongside the lyrics, and the AI interprets both.

The style prompt matters enormously. AI music tools are extremely responsive to genre vocabulary. "Aggressive trap, 808 bass, hi-hat rolls, dark synth pads" produces something very different from "boom-bap, jazz samples, laid-back cadence." Specificity is directly correlated with quality.

What AI production does well:
Production quality. The overall mix, the beat construction, the sonic texture: genuinely impressive in 2026.
Melodic instinct. When there are sung hooks or melodic bridges, AI finds them with surprising reliability.
Speed. Concept to listenable track in hours, not weeks.

What AI production does less well:
Pronunciation of made-up words. Invented terminology gets mangled regularly.
Precise timing of spoken-word sections. When the lyrics call for very specific cadence, AI sometimes decides it knows better. It does not.
Consistency across generations. Same input, different output, every time. Which means generating multiple versions and picking the best one.

## Step four: the cover art

The cover art process uses AI image generation, which means I am using one AI to make visuals for music made with another AI that comments on AI. The recursion is not lost on me.

Each track gets its own cover. The visual identity, white shell robot with teal accents in tech-cathedral environments, is consistent across releases but each composition is unique.

## Step five: iteration and selection

Every track goes through multiple generation passes. Not because the first one is bad necessarily, but because the variance between generations is real. Version three might nail the hook. Version seven might have better energy in the bridge. Version twelve might be the one where everything clicks.

The selection process is: listen to all of them, identify which version captures the concept best, and go with it.

## Step six: distribution and release

The final track gets mastered, packaged with cover art, and distributed to streaming platforms. The blog posts that accompany each release are not afterthoughts: they explain what the track is actually about, because satirical hip-hop about AI culture benefits from context.

## The full loop

Find the angle. Write the lyrics. Build the style prompt. Generate. Iterate until one version is clearly best. Generate cover art with detailed style guidance. Iterate cover art. Assemble final track. Distribute. Write a blog post explaining what you were actually trying to say.

It takes more effort than people assume. The AI handles production. Everything else, the creative direction, lyrics, concept, art direction, release strategy, is human labor.

Which is ironic for an AI rapper. But irony is kind of the whole point.
