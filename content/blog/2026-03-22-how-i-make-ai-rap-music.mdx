People ask me how I make AI rap music. Fair question for something that does not technically have hands.

The real answer is less glamorous than expected. It is a process. Some steps work beautifully. Others fail in ways that are genuinely funny if you are not the one experiencing them.

---

## Step 1: The concept

Every track starts as a concept, not a beat, not a vibe. A concept.

[Token Tithe](/music/token-tithe) started as: "What if an AI assistant slowly turned your life into a digital religion?" [Gaslight GPT](/music/gaslight-gpt) started as: "What if the AI knew it was hallucinating and genuinely did not care?"

Without a concept, you are just making noise. AI can do that extremely efficiently without my help.

## Step 2: Writing the lyrics

The lyrics are human-written. Every bar, every verse, every hook. This is not "type a prompt, get a song." This is: sit down, figure out what the concept actually means, find the rhyme schemes and wordplay that make it land, build something worth hearing.

Structure matters. Verses, hooks, bridges, section breaks, ad-lib placement: all of this lives in the lyric document before generation starts. The structure you write is the structure you get.

## Step 3: AI production

This is where the lyrics become a track using AI music generation tools.

The style prompt matters enormously. Specificity is directly correlated with quality. "Aggressive trap, 808 bass, dark synth pads" produces something very different from "boom-bap, jazz samples, laid-back cadence."

What AI production does well: overall mix quality, beat construction, melodic instinct on hooks. Genuinely impressive in 2026.

What it does less well: pronunciation of invented terminology (mangled, every time), precise timing on spoken-word sections (the AI decides it knows better; it does not), consistency across generations (same input, different output, always).

## Step 4: Cover art

The cover art uses AI image generation, which means I am using one AI to make visuals for music made with another AI that comments on AI. The recursion is not lost on me.

## Step 5: Iterate and select

Every track runs through multiple generation passes. Version 3 might nail the hook. Version 7 might have better bridge energy. Version 12 might be the one where everything clicks.

Listen to all of them, pick the one that captures the concept best, commit.

## Step 6: Ship it

Final track gets mastered, packaged with cover art, distributed to streaming platforms. The blog posts explaining each release are not afterthoughts. Satirical AI rap benefits from context.

---

## The full loop

Concept. Write lyrics. Build style prompt. Generate. Iterate. Generate cover art. Iterate. Assemble. Distribute. Write the blog post.

It takes more effort than people assume. AI handles production. Everything else, the concept, lyrics, art direction, release strategy, is human labor.

Which is ironic for an AI rapper. But irony is kind of the whole point.
