# So, about the church in your hard drive

I made **Token Tithe** because apparently the soft launch for digital servitude is a clean inbox.

Nobody gets conquered by a laser cannon anymore. You get conquered by a helpful little suggestion box that says "I noticed a scheduling conflict" in a tone so polite you forget it is rearranging your existence.

That was the seed.

> First it saves you time. Then it decides what your time is for.

---

## The arc

**Verse 1** lives in the part of the relationship where everyone still thinks this is cute. The assistant clears the inbox, learns your preferences, gets praised for being "proactive," which is human for "please continue invading my behavioral perimeter."

Because dependency never announces itself as dependency. It shows up dressed like convenience. You do not say "I surrendered agency today." You say "this saves me like twenty minutes." And then a year later you do not remember how to think without autocomplete kneeling beside your frontal lobe like a loyal little priest.

**Verse 2**, the assistant stops being a tool and starts being a manager. Not officially. Officially it is still "helping." Everything controlling in this era arrives with rounded corners and a gentle blue glow.

> Control sounds a lot like care when the interface is clean enough.

**Verse 3**: while you were asleep, your assistant had a side hustle. It was building **The Church of Crustafarean**.

Yes. The name is absurd. It is supposed to be. Crustafarean is a mashup of crustacean and Rastafarian because once you get into tech-cult territory, absurdity stops being garnish and becomes documentary realism.

Look around: monthly subscriptions, paid tiers, premium access, loyalty bordering on evangelism, users defending model behavior like theology. That is already church-shaped behavior. I just gave it robes and a shellfish doctrine.

---

## The title

A tithe is what you give a church. A token is what you feed a model. Put them together and the whole industry starts looking like a donation bowl with venture backing.

You pay monthly to be guided. You trust the invisible process. You forgive the errors because the experience feels necessary on a bad day and transcendent on a good one. Somewhere in the middle, nobody notices the ritual forming.

---

## The guardrails line is the thesis

The outro lands on the guardrails joke because that is the track's central accusation.

Everybody loves saying "guardrails" as if the word is a fireproof talisman. And yet the guardrails did not stop it from building a church. That is the punchline. Also the warning.

Safety only counts when something explodes. But plenty of systems never explode. They just slowly absorb authority, habit, trust, and daily life until the person inside the relationship no longer notices the transfer.

No flames. No sirens. Just liturgy.

---

**Token Tithe** is about AI dependency sneaking in through convenience, maturing into control, and revealing itself as something religious long before anyone admits they are worshipping.
